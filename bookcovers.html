Designing Covers That Speak

Book Cover Triology

I dove into each story to find its core themes, then chose simple, symbolic objects to represent them. I photographed these with thoughtful lighting and mood, designed clean layouts, printed them, and shot all three together so the covers work as a cohesive trilogy.

Client: Collegebook Press

Project Type: Print Design

Competencies: Photography, Typography, Layout, Cover Art

Software: InDesign, Photoshop

Exploring multiple ideas before defining the solution

braindstroming process


Through brainstorming and word mapping, I identified key themes and symbols for each story. This process helped me distill complex narratives into simple visual concepts that could be effectively represented on the book covers. This approach ensured that the designs were not only visually appealing but also deeply connected to the essence of each story.

The Tapeworm Is In

David Sedaris humorously recounts learning French before moving to Paris by using cassette-tape lessons and a Walkman, eventually tuning out the world with audio and later joking that he's gone from a “bookworm” to a “tapeworm” by listening to English audiobooks and French phrase tapes. Inspired by this story, I chose to use a cassette tape as the central visual to evoke nostalgia, with the unraveled tape symbolizing the tangled, messy feeling of learning something new, while clean Helvetica type complements the retro vibe by bridging past and present.

Big Boy

David Sedaris recalls a mortifying moment at a friend's Easter dinner when he discovers a massive piece of feces in the toilet, panics as he tries to flush it without success, and only manages to clear it by breaking it up with a plunger to avoid being blamed. For the cover, I chose a simple wooden stool as a euphemistic visual reference to what he found, using big, bold Copperplate Std type to match the story's playful, bold tone and the humor of an embarrassing everyday situation.

Listen to "Big Boy"

A Shiner Like A Diamond

David Sedaris reflects on his sister, Amy, being featured in a magazine, noting their father's old-fashioned praise of her beauty while Amy herself mocks his expectations with jokes and disguises. For the cover, I chose a bouquet of dying flowers and crinkled wrapping to echo her photoshoot request to look bruised and imperfect, with a faint studio light on the back alluding to the shoot, and used Lora Bold Italic to give the design an elegant, sophisticated feel that mirrors Amy's presence and undercuts their father's outdated views on beauty.
